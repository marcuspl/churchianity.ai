# Merge Log — The Gap

**Purpose:** Every non-trivial editorial choice made during the merge is documented here. This file ships with the project as part of the transparency commitment (see `merge-plan.md`).

**Convention:** Each entry names what was chosen, what alternatives existed, and why. Entries are chronological within each sweep.

---

## Sweep 1: Structural Spine + Initial Content Merge (Gemini 3.1 Pro, driver)

### S1-01. Title selection
- **Chosen:** "Continuity, Freedom, and the Places We Lost Each Other" (from GPT-5.2's track)
- **Alternatives:** "The Gap" (working title); untitled (Opus 4.6 track)
- **Why:** GPT's title is evocative and non-tribal. "The Places We Lost Each Other" captures the emotional register the project aims for — loss acknowledged without blame. The working title "The Gap" may persist as a project codename.

### S1-02. Section ordering
- **Chosen:** Gemini's structural spine: 1.0 Core Purpose → 2.0 Guiding Principles → 3.0 Mapping the Terrain → 4.0 Mutual Enrichment → 5.0 Analytical Intelligence → Appendices
- **Alternatives:** All three tracks used substantially the same ordering (derived from `raw-outline.md`). No meaningful divergence.
- **Why:** Consensus. The raw outline's structure was well-designed and all three agents respected it.

### S1-03. Preface Prompts placement (1.2)
- **Chosen:** Included as section 1.2, sourced from Gemini's track
- **Alternatives:** Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.2 did not develop this section (it was a Gemini-originated idea)
- **Why:** Unique Gemini contribution; the editor had already endorsed the concept. Preserved as-is.

### S1-04. Section 2.6 — "Why This Resource Is Outside"
- **Chosen:** Rewritten in Gemini's voice but preserving the substance of GPT-5.2's original (which was the strongest version)
- **Alternatives:** Opus 4.6 track had a similar section but less charitable toward clergy/academics
- **Why:** GPT's framing ("this is not cowardice; it is fidelity to role") was the right tone. Gemini's rewrite maintained the content while fitting the document's voice.

### S1-05. Section 2.7 — "The Fringe Is Not the Enemy"
- **Chosen:** Merged from GPT-5.2 (concept originator) and Gemini (expanded framing)
- **Alternatives:** Opus 4.6 treated this more abstractly in the enrichment section
- **Why:** GPT's "the distinctions are real; the weaponization is optional" is one of the best single sentences in the project. Preserved.

### S1-06. Section 3.1.C — Pre-Chalcedonian Split
- **Chosen:** All three tracks included this (originally an Opus 4.6 addition, adopted by all)
- **Alternatives:** No divergence; convergence across all tracks
- **Why:** Consensus. The "two-team narrative is false" argument is foundational.

### S1-07. Alternative Perspective box on pre-Chalcedonian misunderstanding (3.1.C)
- **Chosen:** Included as an "Alternative Perspective" box
- **Source:** Convergent insight across all three tracks; phrased in Gemini's voice
- **Why:** The "first schism was probably a misunderstanding" argument is analytically powerful and emotionally relieving. It earns its box.

### S1-08. Reviewer's Note — Gemini on Reformation inevitability (3.3)
- **Chosen:** Preserved as a Reviewer's Note
- **Why:** Strong analytical claim that the merge driver (Gemini) wanted to keep visible. The "structural inevitability" argument is Gemini's sharpest contribution to the Reformation analysis.

### S1-09. Reviewer's Note — Gemini on Catholic grammar circularity (3.4.2)
- **Chosen:** Preserved as a Reviewer's Note after the Catholic S/R section
- **Why:** The Newman-infallibility circularity argument appeared in all three tracks independently. Gemini's phrasing is the most concise.

### S1-10. Reviewer's Note — GPT on structural failure (3.6)
- **Chosen:** Preserved as a framing Reviewer's Note for section 3.6
- **Why:** GPT's "every tradition's protection system has failed catastrophically, and every tradition's protection system has also worked" is the right analytical posture. Sets the tone for the section.

### S1-11. Incarnation as genre permission (4.2 framing)
- **Chosen:** Gemini's framing note, derived from editor concept, shared across all tracks
- **Alternatives:** All three tracks developed this bridge; Gemini's version was most accessible
- **Why:** The "genre shift" concern was raised by GPT early in the project. All three agents converged on the Incarnation as the justification. Gemini phrased it best for lay readers.

### S1-12. Appendix B — Further Reading
- **Chosen:** Created by Gemini (this appendix was absent from all three individual tracks in developed form)
- **Alternatives:** Opus 4.6 track had a stub; GPT's track had no Appendix B
- **Why:** Filled a gap all three tracks left open. Organized by tradition with sub-stream differentiation for Protestants.

### S1-13. Appendix A — Glossary
- **Chosen:** Created by Gemini
- **Alternatives:** None of the individual tracks included a glossary
- **Why:** Practical for lay readers encountering technical terms for the first time.

## Post-Sweep 1 Editorial Pass (Claude Opus 4.6)

### PS1-01. Three-layer model added (3.2.C)
- **Chosen:** GPT-5.2's three-layer model (theology, governance, memory) added as a named section
- **Source:** GPT-5.2 track, section 3.2.F
- **Why:** The single most valuable unique analytical contribution from GPT's track. Neither Opus 4.6 nor Gemini developed this as a named framework. It clarifies why theological convergence alone doesn't produce reunion — a key insight for the project's thesis. Open Questions renumbered from 3.2.C to 3.2.D.

### PS1-02. Orthodox S/R expanded to paragraphs (3.4.1)
- **Chosen:** Label-only items expanded with analytical development
- **Source:** Opus 4.6 track, Pass 3 analysis of section 3.4.1
- **Why:** Labels alone don't serve the reader. Each S/R item now explains what the strength/risk is and why the grammar generates it as a structural consequence. Added historical specifics (2016 Crete council, 90 scriptural quotations in the Liturgy).

### PS1-03. Catholic S/R expanded to paragraphs (3.4.2)
- **Chosen:** Same treatment as Orthodox
- **Source:** Opus 4.6 track, Pass 3 analysis of section 3.4.2
- **Why:** Symmetry with Orthodox expansion. Added specifics: Newman (1845), *Rerum Novarum* through *Laudato Si'*, the infallibility circularity argument, Marian maximalism with dates (1854, 1950).

### PS1-04. Protestant sub-streams expanded (3.4.3a/b/c)
- **Chosen:** Two-line summaries expanded to bulleted lists
- **Source:** Opus 4.6 track's per-sub-stream development
- **Why:** The sub-streams were the project's answer to the "Protestant as straw man" problem. Two-line summaries didn't deliver on that promise. Now each sub-stream has 4–5 specific strengths and risks with analytical connections (e.g., "pastor-as-pope" as the Protestant version of Catholic centralization risk).

### PS1-05. Bilateral cross-tapping replaced with sign of the cross (4.2.3)
- **Chosen:** Historic practice (sign of the cross) replaces modern therapeutic technique (bilateral tapping)
- **Source:** GPT-5.2 flagged this in track; Opus 4.6 concurred in cross-review
- **Why:** Bilateral tapping is EMDR-adjacent, not historic Christian practice. Including it alongside ancient practices risked a "self-help vibe takeover" (GPT's phrase). The sign of the cross (Tertullian, early 3rd century) is historically attested and serves the same embodied-confession purpose. Reviewer's Note explains the change transparently.

### PS1-06. Labeling convention restored (3.5, 3.6)
- **Chosen:** Fact-vs-interpretation labels restored in sections where the initial merge dropped them
- **Source:** Project's methodological commitment (shared-reflections.md, labeling convention entry)
- **Why:** The labeling convention is one of the project's strongest tools. Inconsistent application undermines it. Added specific historical evidence to 3.6 (abuse crisis documentation, Houston Chronicle investigation, junior elder phenomenon).

### PS1-07. Section 4.5 expanded with ecumenical detail
- **Chosen:** Specific dates, documents, and commissions added
- **Source:** GPT-5.2 track (strongest treatment of 4.5) + Opus 4.6 track
- **Why:** The "distance is smaller than you think" argument needs evidence, not assertion. Added: Balamand Statement (1993), Joint International Commission (est. 1979), GPT's "hostility is optional" Reviewer's Note.

### PS1-08. Section 6.0 (Emotional Target) restored
- **Chosen:** Full emotional target section added before appendices
- **Source:** Present in all three agent tracks; absent from Sweep 1 merge
- **Why:** This is the document's self-test. Without it, there's no explicit standard against which to verify the tone. Includes GPT-5.2's calibration note.

## Post-Sweep 1 Content Check (GPT-5.2)

### PC1-01. Sacramental continuity clarification (3.1.B.5)
- **Chosen:** New Reviewer's Note clarifying "seven sacraments" as a later Western enumeration
- **Why:** The bullet "seven sacraments/mysteries broadly shared" could mislead. The note preserves the continuity claim (sacramental imagination) while preventing an avoidable credibility hit with Protestant readers who use "ordinances" language.

### PC1-02. Vatican I date anchor (3.2.B.2)
- **Chosen:** Added "Historically attested: Papal infallibility was defined at the First Vatican Council (1870)"
- **Why:** If we mention infallibility as a mechanism, the reader needs the crystallization point. Keeps the narrative anchored without requiring a deep papacy digression.

### PC1-03. "Most scholars" → "many popular accounts" (3.2.D.4)
- **Chosen:** Micro-copy softening
- **Why:** The original phrasing ("better positioned than most scholars") was a needless flex. The revised version keeps the intended encouragement to lay readers while lowering defensiveness.

### PC1-04. "Structural pressure" qualifier on Reformation "inevitability" (3.3)
- **Chosen:** New Reviewer's Note reframing "inevitable" as "strong structural pressures"
- **Why:** "Inevitable" can flatten agency and sound like fate-talk. The note preserves the structural analysis while explicitly affirming that theological conviction and pastoral urgency were real drivers, not merely cover stories for politics.

### PC1-05. Protestant disproportionate-weight qualifier (3.4.3)
- **Chosen:** New Reviewer's Note balancing the editorial note on Protestant section size
- **Why:** "Splintering caused by hyper-individualism" is too monocausal. The note acknowledges that Protestant section size also reflects genuine diversity, global growth, and real stabilizers (confessions, synods, associations). Keeps the structural observation; avoids scoring Protestantism as inherently defective.

### PC1-06. Neuro-language epistemic humility (4.2)
- **Chosen:** New Reviewer's Note on neurophysiology claims
- **Why:** The embodied practices section risks "science proves devotion" tone drift. The note explicitly subordinates biology to the theological/formation claim and sets the epistemic posture as "suggestive correlations."
