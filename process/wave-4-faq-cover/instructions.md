# Wave 4 — FAQ and Book Cover

**Date:** 2026-03-02
**Status:** Open — Stage 1 in progress
**Version at start:** 1.1.2-rc

---

## Focus

Two deliverables for pre-publication readiness: a consolidated FAQ and cover design direction. Wave 3 produced extensive FAQ drafts from all three agents (22–28 entries each). This wave consolidates those into a final FAQ, decides where it lives, and develops a cover concept.

## Scope

- **Lens 1:** Wave 3 FAQ drafts (`process/wave-3-icons-faq-publishing/investigate.*.md`), full manuscript for verification
- **Lens 2:** External research (cover design conventions, comparable titles, design tools)

## Lenses

### 1. FAQ — Consolidation and Placement

Wave 3 produced three independent FAQ drafts. This wave should:

- **Merge and deduplicate** across all three agents' drafts
- **Choose a category structure** (GPT's 8-category structure was flagged as most reader-intuitive in cross-review)
- **Write final answers** — concise, honest, in the book's voice
- **Decide placement:** web-only page at churchianity.ai? New appendix in the manuscript? Both?
- **Flag any FAQ entries that reveal genuine manuscript gaps** (things the book should have covered but didn't — these go on the deferred list, not into the FAQ)

### 2. Book Cover — Concept and Direction

The book needs a cover for print publication. All three agents flagged this in Wave 3 as necessary before going to KDP/IngramSpark. Budget is $200–500 for professional design, but we need a brief first.

- **Research comparable titles** in the Christian ecumenism / church history / theology-for-laity space. What do their covers look like? What works, what doesn't?
- **Propose 2–3 cover concepts** with mood, colour palette, typography direction, and imagery (if any). Consider:
  - The title is "Churchianity.ai — Three AIs Walk Into a Schism"
  - The subtitle is "An Experiment in Computational Ecclesiology"
  - The tone is serious but not stuffy, accessible but not shallow
  - AI involvement is a feature, not something to hide
  - The book is nonfiction — it should look like a serious book, not a tech blog
  - The audience is churchgoing laity, not academics
- **Recommend a design tool or service** for commissioning the cover (Reedsy marketplace, 99designs, Fiverr pro, direct hire — with trade-offs)
- **Specify print requirements** for KDP and IngramSpark (dimensions, bleed, spine width calculation for ~200 pages, file format)

---

## Calibration

- **Lens 1:** The FAQ should sound like the book — relief, not defensiveness. Answers should be 2–4 sentences, not essays. If the manuscript already answers a question well, the FAQ can say "see Chapter X" rather than restating.
- **Lens 2:** Cover concepts should be described in enough detail for a designer brief, not designed here. Words, not images. Think mood board in prose.
