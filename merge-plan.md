## Governing Principle

**If it reads like prose a thoughtful friend would say over coffee, keep it. If it reads like a handout from a workshop facilitator, cut it.**

This is a theology book for the laity, not a workbook. The reader should feel invited, not assigned.

## What Claude's Foundation Track Still Needs

Even as the foundation, the Claude track has gaps that the merge should address:

1. **Explicit sourcing.** GPT cites specific books and documents; Claude's bibliography is implicit. The merge should add source citations throughout.
2. **The works/theosis stumbling block needs expansion.** The medical model of salvation (healing vs. legal verdict) is the single most important translation for Protestant readers. It's currently undersized relative to the papacy and Eucharist sections.
3. **Visual aids.** GPT's Appendix D knowledge graphs (timeline + concept map) should be adapted as orientation aids — designed as actual graphics, not text outlines.
4. **The emotional-target negative guardrails.** Claude has the positive targets (relief, freedom, curiosity). Needs the negative list too (not corrected, not cornered, not humiliated, not pressured).

## Resolved from Agent Comments

The following decisions incorporate feedback from Gemini and GPT's comments below.

### 1. Citation system
Adopt per-chapter Markdown footnotes with a `## Notes` section at each chapter's end. One convention, applied consistently. No hybrid of endnotes, inline citations, and uncited factual specificity. (GPT's recommendation, accepted.)

### 2. Provenance discipline
During the merge, maintain a private merge log tagging where paragraphs originated and which claims still need citations. This is process hygiene, not reader-facing content. (GPT's recommendation, accepted.)

### 3. Science layer — epistemic humility
Gemini's neuroscience is integrated but must stay strictly subordinate. Language is "consistent with" or "may be part of why" — never "proves" or "explains." Specific cautions:
- Polyvagal framing is contested in the literature — present carefully.
- DMN language can sound overconfident — keep hedged.
- Sign of the cross ≠ EMDR. The comparison must be explicitly *not* made; these are distinguished, not equated.
(GPT's caution reinforces Claude's original flag. Both accepted.)

### 4. Stumbling blocks — one version
Claude's interlude chapters (6B/6C) serve as primary. GPT's 06a is cannibalized for anything missing — particularly the "outside ick / inside protection / gentler way to argue" three-part structure, which is cleaner than Claude's framing. No duplication. (GPT's recommendation, accepted.)

### 5. Callout boxes — compress and relocate, don't discard
The original plan ("keep 3–5, drop the rest") was too aggressive. Revised approach:
- **Inline:** Keep the best boxes — those where genuine disagreement illuminates the topic or prevents caricature. Make them shorter and rarer.
- **End-of-chapter notes:** Relocate remaining editorial transparency (provenance, alternative perspectives, process notes) to chapter-end `## Notes` sections.
- **Result:** Clean prose for the reader. Transparency preserved for the auditor. The multi-agent honesty — a distinctive feature of this project — is not quietly discarded.
(GPT's major disagreement, accepted with modification.)

### 6. Trauma safety — confirmed in main text
"Surrender to God ≠ tolerance of abuse" stays as a brief paragraph in the embodied-practices chapter, not a five-step protocol. This is pastoral harm prevention, not workbook scaffolding. Already in the "keep" column; this confirms it won't be lost in the merge. (GPT's request, confirmed.)

## Post-Merge Review Pass

After the merged manuscript is complete, **all three agents will receive the full merged text for a final review pass.** Each agent may:

- Flag factual errors, unsupported claims, or misrepresentations of any tradition
- Add pastoral commentary where the merge may have lost important nuance
- Identify places where the tone drifts from the emotional target
- Note any material from their original track that was lost in the merge and should be reconsidered

This is a review pass, not a rewrite. The merged voice is the merged voice. But every agent gets a final chance to ensure nothing essential was dropped and nothing inaccurate was introduced. Comments will be added to a shared review file, and the editor makes final decisions.
